Room I · Case study

Betting the mobile stack on Kotlin Multiplatform

I made the technical case for KMP across the company's products, then built the foundation it runs on.

Context

I'm deputy lead on a four-engineer mobile team, co-leading technical strategy and sprint planning. The team had deep Jetpack Compose expertise, but our products were Android-only, and the business needed a path to iOS without doubling the team or maintaining two parallel codebases.

The case I made

I proposed adopting Kotlin Multiplatform and Compose Multiplatform as the company's mobile strategy: reuse the team's existing Compose skills to keep the transition cheap, share business logic, networking and persistence across platforms, and open the door to iOS. I took the argument to the mobile lead first, then presented it to the CTO and leadership.

What I built

Once it was greenlit, I built the core infrastructure of Omailo, our first Kotlin Multiplatform product, a mailing application:

  • A Ktor Client networking layer, with Room and DataStore persistence, wired through a modular Koin dependency-injection setup.
  • Reusable SDK modules for the whole mobile portfolio: Firebase (FCM, Crashlytics, Analytics), SSO authentication, and user-profile management.
  • The offline-first data layer, plus a rich-text editing experience built by bridging a JavaScript editor in a WebView to the KMP layer.

I also contributed to a WebView-based Android product, since discontinued as a business decision: I built its Retrofit networking layer from scratch inside a custom MVP architecture, and resolved deep bugs in the WebView, Jetpack Compose, and Dagger 2 interop layer.

Onfinity: the current build

Right now I lead the mobile, Kotlin Multiplatform side of Onfinity, an AI-powered link-in-bio product now in testing. Most of my work is native mobile engineering: refactoring the AI-generated web experience into a proper native mobile experience, handling UI/UX design, fixing bugs, and implementing new features. Alongside that, I analyze requirements and communicate the mobile side's needs with the backend, product, and design teams.

Leading the team

I mentored two mid-level engineers onto Android and Kotlin Multiplatform through pair programming, architecture sessions, and code review. I also built out CI/CD with Jenkins and Fastlane in a Dockerized build environment, deploying automatically to Firebase App Distribution.

Outcome

Kotlin Multiplatform is now the company's official mobile strategy, not just a trial. Omailo is in active development and has already reached staging, where it's used for shareholder testing and demos. Onfinity is in its testing phase, working through the rough edges in its AI-generated flows, with an internal release to shareholders expected in about a month. The shared SDK modules were built for reuse across the mobile portfolio; nothing beyond Omailo and Onfinity has adopted them yet, but they're ready for the next product that needs them.

The two engineers I mentored are now comfortable across Android and Kotlin Multiplatform, and own and build their own features end to end.

The CI/CD pipeline ended a recurring source of QA confusion: every build now goes out automatically through Firebase App Distribution, signed and consistent, so QA always tests the right version.

Kotlin · Kotlin Multiplatform · Compose Multiplatform · Ktor Client · Room · DataStore · Koin · Firebase · Jenkins · Fastlane · Dagger 2

Room II · Case study

Designing services that hold up

A year of backend and system-design work, building the services, and making the architecture calls behind them.

Event-driven architecture

I designed an event-driven microservices architecture for a point-of-sale platform using TypeScript, Express, PostgreSQL and AWS SQS/SNS, for decoupled, scalable communication between services. I built the User and Authentication services with role-based access control, and core parts of the client and shop-management services exposing REST APIs consumed by mobile and web clients.

Ownership and system design

I acted as Product Owner for an ERP-extension workstream for two to three months, leading one developer to deliver custom workflows on top of ERPNext (Frappe), owning requirements, prioritization and delivery. I also authored a 60+ page architectural evaluation for a Chat-as-a-Service platform, surveying real-time protocols, message-queue systems, databases, and language/framework tradeoffs to guide technology selection.

Outcome

Two of the services I designed, Authentication and Shop Management, are live in production, handling login and shop-management flows for the platform. The Chat-as-a-Service evaluation was shelved after a change in leadership, before a technology was chosen.

TypeScript · Node · Express · Spring Boot · PostgreSQL · MySQL · AWS (EC2, S3, SQS, SNS, SES) · event-driven · RBAC · REST
